How Until works

Until turns a price and a savings rate into a date. No budgeting system, no account sync — just the thing you want, the amount you can set aside, and the math in between. This page walks through both ways to use it.

Estimate your timeline

This is the default mode — use it when you just want to know how long it'll take, starting from today.

  1. Under The purchase, enter the item's name, quantity, currency, and unit price.
  2. Under The plan, choose how often you'll save, the currency you're saving in, and the amount per period.
  3. Select Calculate.
  4. A result stub appears below the form with the date math worked out — how many days, weeks, months, or years until you've saved enough.

Build a savings plan

Use this mode when you want a concrete start and end date to commit to, rather than a duration counted from today.

  1. Select Generate savings plan. A Starting date field appears in the form.
  2. Fill in the same purchase and plan details as before, plus the date you intend to start saving.
  3. Select Calculate. The result now shows a starting date and an ending date side by side, with a plain-language summary underneath.
  4. Select Back to default mode at any time to return to the simple duration estimate.

Reading your results

The large figure in the result stub is your estimated saving period — for example, 6 weeks. Hover or tap it to see that same period converted into other units, like days or months, in case one of them is easier to plan around.

If your savings amount doesn't divide evenly into the total price, you'll see a line noting the extra amount needed on the final deposit — this is the remainder after your last full installment.

If your savings amount already covers the total cost on its own, there's nothing to calculate: you can already afford it.

Currencies and rates

Until supports six currencies for both the item price and your savings: Philippine Peso, US Dollar, Euro, Chinese Yuan, Japanese Yen, and British Pound. If your price and savings currencies differ, the total is converted using a fixed reference rate built into the app.

These rates are a snapshot, not a live feed — treat the result as a close estimate rather than an exact market conversion, especially for larger amounts.

Questions

Why does the unit change from weeks to months when I hover the result?

The hover card just re-expresses the same length of time in other units, so you can pick whichever one is easiest to plan around — a payday cadence, a calendar month, and so on. The underlying answer doesn't change.

What does "extra amount needed" mean?

Until rounds your timeline down to a whole number of saving periods. If that leaves a remainder — say your price doesn't divide evenly by your savings amount — it's shown as a small extra deposit needed on top of your regular schedule.

Can I save in one currency and price the item in another?

Yes. Set the price currency and the savings currency independently, and Until converts between them automatically using its built-in reference rates.

Does Until store or sync anything?

No. Nothing is saved between visits — every calculation starts fresh from what you enter in the form.
